Early Life

Stephen William Hawking was born on January 8, 1942, in Oxford, England. Despite a modest upbringing, he exhibited an early passion for science. In 1959, at the age of 17, he enrolled at University College, Oxford, where he pursued physics and graduated with a first-class BA degree. He later commenced his graduate studies at Trinity Hall, Cambridge, focusing on general relativity and cosmology. At 21, Hawking was diagnosed with a rare form of motor neurone disease (ALS), a condition that gradually paralyzed him over the years. Despite this, he continued his academic pursuits with unwavering determination.

Achievements

Hawking's contributions to theoretical physics and cosmology are monumental:

  • Singularity Theorems: Collaborated with Roger Penrose to develop theorems predicting the universe's beginning as a singularity, supporting the Big Bang theory.
  • Hawking Radiation: Theorized that black holes emit radiation due to quantum effects near their event horizons, a phenomenon now known as Hawking radiation.
  • No-Boundary Proposal: Proposed that the universe has no boundaries in imaginary time, suggesting a self-contained universe without a singular starting point.
  • Top-Down Cosmology: Suggested that the universe's present state determines its past histories, offering a fresh perspective on cosmological models.

Beyond his research, Hawking authored the bestseller A Brief History of Time, aiming to explain complex cosmological concepts to the general public. The book remained on the Sunday Times bestseller list for a record-breaking 237 weeks, captivating readers worldwide.

Legacy

Stephen Hawking's legacy extends beyond his scientific discoveries. He demonstrated that physical limitations need not hinder intellectual achievement. His life story inspired the 2014 film The Theory of Everything, which portrayed his early years, academic endeavors, and personal challenges. Hawking's resilience and contributions have left an indelible mark on science and popular culture, motivating countless individuals to explore the mysteries of the universe.
